Trading strategies

Three day-ahead strategies, built in Python on realised EPEX auction prices (2019–2026). Each links to a full two-page tearsheet with method, sensitivity and risk. Figures are per unit of position, costs included.

Battery day-ahead arbitrage — structural, LP / HiGHS

A 1 MW / 2 MWh battery dispatched optimally against each day's auction curve. The edge is structural: more solar deepens the midday trough, so the spread a battery captures widens each year. Day-ahead only is the floor.

€85.9k / MW (DE-LU, 2025) · €88.4k / MW (CZ) · €89.9k 2026 YTD annualised

Solar cannibalisation spread — structural, volume-neutral

The same physics without owning a battery: sell the midday block, buy the evening block, equal size. No directional price exposure, only the intraday shape. The March–October solar season carries almost all of it.

+€104/MWh (DE-LU, 2025 Mar–Oct) · +€70/MWh 7-yr average · 100% Mar–Oct win rate, 4 years
Read the tearsheet →
CZ–DE cross-border spread — statistical, shown sceptically

Fades dislocations in the coupled CZ / DE-LU spread back to parity. Shown with a paper-vs-tradable honesty check: the execution lag and costs roughly halve it. I treat this as a research lead, not a production strategy.

1.43 tradable Sharpe · 1.1–1.8 bootstrap 90% CI · 2.42 paper Sharpe (ceiling)
