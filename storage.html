Storage feasibility

A longer piece of work: a full techno-economic model, grounded in the same battery backtest as the trading strategies.

Substation to storage — techno-economic, NPV / IRR, 3 scenarios + sensitivities

Is it worth turning decommissioned Czech substations (transformovny / rozvodny) into grid batteries instead of demolishing them? The edge is the existing grid connection: it skips the two-to-four-year connection queue and the capex that comes with it. In the base case that is decisive, not incremental — reuse earns a 12% IRR while the identical greenfield battery barely breaks even (roughly NPV-zero at an 8% discount rate). The reuse advantage is stable across scenarios because it is a capex-and-timing difference, not a revenue bet. A conditional yes: only for the larger distribution and transmission sites, and realistically built by the grid owner or a connection-holding partner — the pattern now playing out at Chvaletice, Kladno and Vítkovice.

+€2.6m reuse NPV edge (base case) · 12% vs 8% IRR reuse vs greenfield · 20 MW / 40 MWh representative site

Day-ahead arbitrage is taken from my own LP battery backtest on realised CZ prices; balancing revenue, capex and the connection saving are parameterised from 2025–26 Czech market data, with explicit scenarios and sensitivities. Rev. 2 followed an adversarial model review: capacity is allocated between the merchant and balancing markets rather than double-counted, greenfield capex is phased, and ancillary rates are calibrated to 2026 auctions. The full model code is included in the appendix.
